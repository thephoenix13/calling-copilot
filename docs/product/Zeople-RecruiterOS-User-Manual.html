01

Getting Started

First-time login and what to expect.

Zeople RecruiterOS is an AI-powered multi-tenant recruiting platform. Each company (your agency) has its own users, jobs, candidates, and pipeline. Users belong to a company; data never leaks across companies. Inside a company, the 6-role hierarchy determines what each person can see and do.

Login URL (dev)
http://localhost:5174/
Backend API
http://localhost:3000

On first login you land on either the Home / Welcome Dashboard (recruiter-side roles) or the Hiring Manager Portal (HMs). The bottom-left of the sidebar shows your name and role chip — for an Owner this reads OWNER.

02

Roles at a glance

Six roles, ordered top-down by authority.
Owner
Top of an agency. Billing, all settings, full visibility, manages the team.
Team Lead
Sees all of the team's pipeline work, can review QA across recruiters, can assign jobs.
Sr Recruiter
Senior IC. Same operational scope as Recruiter, plus can manage assignees / hiring managers on jobs.
Recruiter
Day-to-day operator. Runs pipelines, makes calls, sources candidates, sends assessments.
Sourcer
Lightweight role. Only sources candidates (Step 3 of pipeline). No decisions, no calls, no assessments.
Hiring Manager
External stakeholder. Sees only jobs they're attached to. Reviews shortlisted candidates and submits feedback. Lands on a separate portal — no recruiter sidebar.

The sidebar — what each role sees

Nav items hide automatically based on the signed-in role.

The left sidebar dynamically filters based on your role. Below is what each role sees, top to bottom:

OWNER / TEAM LEAD
  • Home, Calling CoPilot, Job Management, Candidate Database, JD Enhancer
  • Pipeline: Pipeline Sessions, Post Offer Follow-Up
  • Evaluation: Video Interviews, MCQ Assessments, Coding Assessments
  • Insights: Reports & Analytics, Recruiter QA, Activity Feed
  • Admin: Settings (Owner only)
SR RECRUITER / RECRUITER
  • Home, Calling CoPilot, Job Management, Candidate Database, JD Enhancer
  • Pipeline: Pipeline Sessions, Post Offer Follow-Up
  • Evaluation: Video Interviews, MCQ Assessments, Coding Assessments
  • Insights: Reports & Analytics, Recruiter QA
  • (no Activity Feed, no Settings)
SOURCER
  • Home, Job Management (read-only), Candidate Database
  • Pipeline: Pipeline Sessions (Step 3 only)
  • Insights: Reports & Analytics, Recruiter QA (own data)
  • (no Calling CoPilot, no Evaluation, no POFU, no JD Enhancer is shown but read-allowed)
HIRING MANAGER
  • Lands on the dedicated Hiring Manager Portal (no recruiter sidebar).
  • Sees: their attached jobs, candidates per job, AI scores, feedback form.
  • Cannot reach any recruiter route, even via direct URL.
05

Module reference

14 modules across the platform. The colored eyebrow on each card shows the audience.
Home
Everyone (recruiter-side roles)
Personalised greeting, recent-activity snapshot, KPI cards, quick links into modules.
  • First screen after login.
  • Numbers refresh on load.
  • Hiring Managers don't see this — they land on the HM portal directly.
Calling CoPilot
Recruiters, Sr Recruiters, Team Leads, Owners
Live AI coaching during recruiter calls. Real-time transcription + suggested follow-up questions. Generates QA + Candidate reports after every call.
  • After a call, two reports are saved: a 7-dimension recruiter QA scorecard and a candidate evaluation.
  • The "Demo Vid" button has been removed — to demo the flow to someone, run a real (or simulated) call from inside a Pipeline Session.
  • Live AI Suggestions appear in the right panel ~1 second after each candidate response. Speed has been tuned for a fast walkthrough.
  • Sourcers and Hiring Managers cannot access this module.
Job Management
Everyone except Hiring Managers see all company jobs (HMs see only attached ones).
Create and manage job postings; assign teammates; attach external Hiring Managers; review HM feedback per candidate.
  • List view has a My Jobs / All Jobs filter and a Lead column (the primary recruiter on each job).
  • Job Detail card has FOUR collaboration panels: Skills, Team (assignees), Hiring Managers, and Hiring Manager Feedback.
  • Adding a "Lead" assignee auto-demotes the previous Lead to Collaborator (one Lead per job).
  • Owner / Team Lead / Sr Recruiter can manage assignees and HMs. Recruiter can edit job content but not assignees.
  • Sourcers and Hiring Managers see the list but no Edit/Delete/+ New buttons.
Candidate Database
Everyone except Hiring Managers (HMs use the per-job candidate list in their portal).
Searchable database of candidates with contact details, experience, skills, resume text, and history. AI Content Check available on any resume.
  • Sourcers can ADD candidates but not delete them — they're the primary sourcing role.
  • Resume parser auto-extracts structured fields from PDF / DOCX uploads.
  • Candidates are scoped to your company — different agencies don't share a pool.
JD Enhancer
Recruiters, Sr Recruiters, Team Leads, Owners (Sourcer can read only).
Paste a raw job description, get five ready-to-use assets back: Formatted JD, Recruiter Brief, Clarification Questions for the HM, Reachout Material, and Sourcing Keywords.
  • Each asset has a copy button.
  • Generated assets are bound to the session if you used it from inside a Pipeline Session, or saved standalone otherwise.
Pipeline Sessions
Recruiters / Sr Recruiters / Team Leads / Owners run them. Sourcers can act on Step 3 only. HMs see attached-job sessions read-only.
7-step agentic hiring workflow: Select JD → Enhance JD → Source Candidates → Recruiter Screening → Assessment Round → Decision → Pipeline Tracker.
  • Step 1 Select the job for this drive (binds session to job_id).
  • Step 2 Enhance JD — AI generates the 5 assets reused everywhere downstream.
  • Step 3 Source candidates from the database; AI scores resume match and runs an AI Content Check.
  • Step 4 Recruiter screening — Pass / On Hold / Reject. Optionally launch a live screening call (Calling CoPilot opens with JD + resume context).
  • Step 5 Send Video Interview / MCQ / Coding assessments. AI evaluates submissions.
  • Step 6 Set interview level (L1/L2/L3), decision (Proceed/Pool), schedule the next round.
  • Step 7 Kanban board — move candidates through Selected / On Hold / Rejected.
  • Sourcer view: a purple banner above the steps explains the role; Steps 4–8 buttons are disabled.
  • Steps unlock progressively — you can't jump ahead until the prior step has enough data.
Video Interviews
Recruiters and up. HMs can read evaluations on attached jobs. Sourcers cannot access.
Async video interview templates. Send invites, candidates record on their own time, AI evaluates each response across 5 competencies.
  • Question Generator: AI builds a mix of behavioral / technical / situational questions from the JD.
  • Evaluator: scores Communication, Technical Skills, Problem Solving, Leadership, Cultural Fit + a hiring recommendation.
  • Results auto-flow into Pipeline Session Step 5.
MCQ & Coding Assessments
Recruiters and up. Sourcers and HMs cannot access.
Build pre-screen tests. Candidates take them on a personal invite link. AI evaluator runs after submission.
  • MCQ: 4-option multiple choice with topic + difficulty tags. Configurable time limit and pass score.
  • Coding: in-browser code editor with starter code. Three problem types: write / fix / complete.
  • Both produce an AI evaluation alongside the raw score.
Post Offer Follow-Up (POFU)
Recruiters and up. Sourcers and HMs cannot access.
Once a candidate accepts an offer, automated check-in emails fire at the right intervals. Tracks the offer-to-Day-1 lifecycle and flags drop risk.
  • Lifecycle states: offer_accepted → resigned → BGV → confirmed → joined / dropped.
  • Risk score (0–100) and risk level (low / medium / high) per candidate.
  • Emails are AI-generated based on the trigger reason (weekly check-in, silence follow-up, DOJ reminder, BGV request, etc.).
  • Auto-pauses if the candidate replies; resumes on silence.
Reports & Analytics
Owner / Team Lead see company-wide aggregations. Everyone else sees their own work. HMs cannot access.
Eight-tab analytics across the full hiring funnel — Pipeline, Efficiency, Candidates, Assessments, Video Interviews, Post-Offer, Activity, By Job.
  • KPI strip on every view: Active Jobs, Avg Time-to-Hire, Selection Rate, Pipeline Sessions, Candidates, Assessments Done, Video Interviews, POFU, Calls Made.
  • Owner / Team Lead numbers reflect the whole team. Recruiter / Sourcer numbers reflect just their own work.
Recruiter QA
Owner / Team Lead see all company QA reports. Recruiters / Sr Recruiters see their own. Sourcers and HMs cannot access.
Aggregator for per-call QA scorecards. KPI strip + searchable list + drill-down to the full Per-Call QA Report.
  • Coaching surface — Team Leads can spot patterns ("recruiter X is consistently weak on Closing Effectiveness").
  • Each scorecard has 7 weighted dimensions, red flags, and weak/better coaching nudges grounded in the call transcript.
Activity Feed
Owner / Team Lead only. Hidden in nav for everyone else.
Append-only timeline of company actions: job created/updated/deleted, assignees added/removed, HMs attached/detached, HM feedback submitted, session candidate state changes.
  • Filter chips: All / Jobs / Sessions / Pipeline updates / Candidate Feedback.
  • Each row shows colored avatar with initials, who did what, the entity reference, and a relative timestamp ("12 min ago", "3 hr ago", "2 d ago").
  • Used for compliance, dispute resolution, and Team Lead coaching context.
Settings (Owner only)
Owner only. Hidden for everyone else.
Three tabs: Company Info, Team Management, My Account.
  • Team Management — invite recruiters by email, assign roles (Team Lead / Sr Recruiter / Recruiter / Sourcer / Hiring Manager), activate/deactivate accounts.
  • Company Info — company name, industry, website, logo URL, contact email.
  • My Account — show current sign-in (with role pill), update display name, change password.
  • New team members inherit the Owner's company.
Hiring Manager Portal
Hiring Managers only. Lands here automatically — no recruiter sidebar.
Slimmed-down stakeholder view: jobs they're attached to, candidates the recruiter has shortlisted on each, AI evaluation scores, a feedback form.
  • Layout: navy header with the Zeople logo, "Hiring Manager Portal" label, sign-out button. Body: jobs grid → click into a job → candidates with scores + feedback form.
  • Feedback form — 5-level recommendation pills (Strong Yes / Yes / Maybe / No / Strong No) + a free-form notes textarea.
  • Submissions persist and prefill on next visit.
  • HM cannot navigate to /jobs, /candidates, /sessions etc. directly — even via URL — for jobs they aren't attached to.
06

Hiring Manager Portal — focus

A separate UI for external client stakeholders.

The HM Portal exists to let your clients (the people you're recruiting for) review your shortlist and give feedback — without giving them recruiter-level access. When a user with role hiring_manager logs in, they bypass the recruiter AppShell entirely and land on a dedicated portal.

The HM only sees jobs they're explicitly attached to (via Job Detail → Hiring Managers panel → + Add Hiring Manager). Inside each job they see:

  • Job basics — title, client, location, required skills.
  • Shortlisted candidates (those past Step 4 screening) with: name, current title/company, match %, VI score, pipeline status.
  • Recruiter's comment (if the recruiter wrote a vi_review note).
  • A feedback form per candidate: 5-level recommendation pill + free-form notes.

Submitted feedback flows back to the recruiter UI in two places: the Hiring Manager Feedback panel on Job Detail (grouped by candidate) and an hm.feedback.submit entry in the Activity Feed.

07

Common workflows

Step-by-step for the most frequent tasks.
Run a hiring drive end-to-end
Recruiter / Sr Recruiter
  1. Job Management → click an active job → ensure you (or a teammate) are listed as Lead in the Team panel.
  2. Pipeline Sessions → + New Session → pick the job and a name.
  3. Step 1 confirms the JD selection. Step 2 click Generate Assets to produce the 5 sourcing aids.
  4. Step 3 click Add Candidates to source from the database. AI scores resume match + flags AI-written resumes.
  5. Step 4 mark candidates Pass / On Hold / Reject. Optionally click Screen via Call to open Calling CoPilot with JD + resume context. After the call, the QA + Candidate reports save automatically.
  6. Step 5 pick a sub-mode (Video / MCQ / Coding) and send invites. When candidates submit, click Evaluate for AI scoring.
  7. Step 6 set interview level (L1/L2/L3) + Proceed/Pool decision + schedule the next interview.
  8. Step 7 use the Kanban board to move candidates to Selected / On Hold / Rejected as panels report back.
  9. On offer acceptance, the candidate auto-transfers to POFU.
Invite a teammate (Owner)
Owner only
  1. Sidebar → Settings → Team Management tab.
  2. Click + Add Member.
  3. Enter email, display name, password (min 6 chars), and pick a role from the dropdown.
  4. Click Create Account. The new user can log in immediately with the password you set.
  5. For Sourcers / Hiring Managers, the sidebar they see will be limited automatically — you don't need to configure visibility.
Bring in a Hiring Manager and review their feedback
Owner / Team Lead / Sr Recruiter
  1. (Once) Settings → Team Management → invite the HM with role Hiring Manager.
  2. Job Management → open the relevant job → scroll to Hiring Managers panel → click + Add Hiring Manager → pick from the modal.
  3. The HM now sees that one job in their portal when they sign in. Recruiters can keep working as normal.
  4. After the HM submits feedback on candidates, refresh the same Job Detail page — the Hiring Manager Feedback panel populates with each entry: HM name, recommendation pill (Strong Yes / Yes / etc.), italic notes, and timestamp.
Coach a recruiter using Recruiter QA
Owner / Team Lead
  1. Sidebar → Insights → Recruiter QA.
  2. KPI strip shows team aggregates: Calls Reviewed, Avg QA Score, Needs Coaching %, Weakest Dimension.
  3. Sort the list by Lowest Score First to find calls that need attention.
  4. Click any row to open the full Per-Call QA Report — 8 weighted dimensions, red flags, weak vs. better coaching prompts grounded in the transcript.
  5. Use the coaching nudges as discussion points in your 1:1.
Use the Sourcer flow
Sourcer
  1. Login. Sidebar shows: Home, Job Management, Candidate Database, Pipeline Sessions, JD Enhancer, Reports & Analytics, Recruiter QA. (No Calling CoPilot, no Evaluation modules, no POFU — by design.)
  2. Open a Pipeline Session → land directly on Step 3. A purple banner reads: "Sourcer view — you can add candidates and run AI evaluations in Step 3. Steps 4–8 are read-only or disabled."
  3. Source candidates using the database, manual add, or bulk upload. AI scores match and flags AI-written resumes.
  4. Hand off to a Recruiter — they'll handle Steps 4 onward.
Audit company activity (Owner / Team Lead)
Owner / Team Lead
  1. Sidebar → Insights → Activity Feed.
  2. Use the filter chips to narrow: All / Jobs / Sessions / Pipeline updates / Candidate Feedback.
  3. Each row tells you who did what, when, and links to the entity by ID.
  4. Useful for compliance, dispute resolution, and "what happened" investigations.

Test plan for the QA team

Structured scenarios to verify the platform end-to-end.
Login & roles
  • Log in as Pratik (Owner). Sidebar should show all modules including Insights → Activity Feed and Admin → Settings. Bottom-left chip shows "OWNER".
  • Log in as Divakar (Owner). Should see same modules. Should NOT see Pratik's jobs / candidates / activity (cross-tenant isolation).
  • Settings → Team Management → invite a Team Lead. Log in as them. Activity Feed visible. Settings hidden. They see the team's sessions, calls, QA reports, /reports/summary numbers.
  • Invite a Recruiter. Sidebar narrower (no Activity Feed, no Settings, no Users). They only see their own pipeline data.
  • Invite a Sourcer. Sidebar very narrow. Pipeline session opens to Step 3 with banner; Steps 4–8 disabled.
  • Invite a Hiring Manager. They land on the HM portal — no recruiter sidebar. Empty state until you attach them to a job.
Permissions hardening
  • As a Recruiter, try to delete a job from Job Management. Button hidden. (API call would also 403.)
  • As a Sourcer, the +New Job button should be hidden, Edit/Delete row actions show "Read-only".
  • As a Sourcer, sidebar items for Calling CoPilot, Video Interviews, MCQ Assessments, Coding Assessments, POFU, Settings should all be hidden.
  • As a Hiring Manager, you can't reach /jobs, /candidates, /pofu, etc. — even by URL. The only routes available are /hm/*.
Job collaboration
  • Open a Job Detail. Confirm the Team panel shows a Lead (with orange chip), and that you can add a Collaborator from the modal.
  • Try adding a teammate as Lead. Verify the previous Lead auto-demotes to Collaborator (one Lead per job).
  • Try removing the LAST assignee. Should be blocked with "Cannot remove the last assignee. Add another first."
  • Open the Hiring Managers panel → + Add Hiring Manager → pick a teammate with role HM → confirm chip appears.
HM portal end-to-end
  • Log in as the HM. See only the job(s) they're attached to.
  • Click a job → see candidate list with match %, VI score, status.
  • Submit feedback (Strong Yes + a note). Refresh — the form prefills.
  • Switch to the Owner login → open the same job → confirm the Hiring Manager Feedback panel shows the feedback you just submitted.
Activity audit
  • As Owner, perform a few actions: create a job, attach an HM, submit HM feedback (in the HM portal), delete a session.
  • Open Activity Feed. Confirm each action appears as a separate row with the right action label and entity reference.
  • Use the filter chips to narrow to "Jobs" only — verify the job-related rows isolate correctly.
Demo data exploration
  • Job Management list → confirm 13 jobs exist (10 in Pratik's workspace, 3 in Divakar's).
  • Recruiter QA → confirm 9–18 QA reports exist (varying scores 30–92, varying verdicts).
  • POFU → confirm 12+ candidates across all 6 lifecycle states.
Two automated test scripts also exist for engineers: node scripts/smoke.js (59 invariants) and node scripts/selftest.js (6 deeper probes). Run after any change.
09

Known limitations

Things to know before pinging engineering.
  • Demo Vid button removed — to demo the platform to someone, run a real screening call from a Pipeline Session.
  • No magic-link auth for HMs — HMs use a password set by the Owner during invite. They'll need to be told their password.
  • Sr Recruiter is functionally identical to Recruiter except they have `jobs.assign` capability — they can manage assignees + HMs without needing Owner/Team Lead.
  • Single Owner per company — there's no UI flow to transfer ownership yet. Workaround: Owner manually edits via /settings/team.
  • No CSV / Excel export from Reports & Analytics yet — flagged in the roadmap.
  • No source-of-hire tracking, offer acceptance analytics, or cost-per-hire — these are gaps vs. enterprise ATS, intentionally not in scope yet.
  • Activity log captures user actions, not AI-only side effects — e.g., a VI evaluation generated by AI is NOT a separate entry in the feed (it's implied by the candidate state change that follows).
  • Team Lead view of /reports/summary treats "team" as everyone in the same company. There's no concept of "my direct reports" vs "the whole company" yet.
